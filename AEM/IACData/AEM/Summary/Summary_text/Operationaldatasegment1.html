We recognize the close links between environmental stresses and health. This includes the many ways in which climate change affects human health, such as driving increases in respiratory and cardiovascular diseases and accelerating the spread of vector-borne diseases like malaria. According to the WHO, climate change impacts are already harming health through air pollution, disease, extreme weather events, forced displacement, food insecurity and pressures on mental health. In fact, the WHO reports that every year, environmental factors take the lives of around 13 million people.
